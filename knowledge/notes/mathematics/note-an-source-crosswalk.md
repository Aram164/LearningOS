# Analysis source crosswalk (narrative)

Keyed to **the skript** (`unser skript.pdf`, 258 pp) — THE exam-defining source;
everything else exists to make the skript readable and its proofs reproducible.

## The layer model

**Layer 1 — intuition/calc fluency** (Professor Leonard, 3B1B Essence of
Calculus, Strang, Thomas): watch/read BEFORE the skript when a topic is new —
never as a substitute. **Layer 2 — the skript** ⭐: definitions, theorem
statements, notation; the Klausur comes from here. **Layer 3 — rigor/proof
technique** (MIT 18.100A mapped lectures, Abbott, Grieser as the DE Abbott,
Lebl, Ross, Rudin for the hardest proofs only): watch mapped lectures only,
never linearly; pick ONE book per stuck proof. **DE quick-help** (Daniel Jung,
MathePeter): 5-minute unblocks for German terms/techniques, not systematic
study. **Drill** (Forster-Wessoly ⭐, Fritzsche as the gentlest tier, Deitmar
for fresh problems, the Drill-Lösungen folder): the exam points live here.

## Rules that made this work

The gentle→rigorous ladder means: when a proof stalls, drop a layer, don't
grind. `kleine_beweise.pdf` (the HU tutors' own exam-prep proof list) is the
single most exam-shaped artifact — EVERY proof in it is AN.X material, and
Ableitinger/Herrmann teaches exactly the skill of reading and reproducing such
Musterlösungen. Rodriguez's xⁿ-on-[0,1] counterexample (18.100A L23–L24) is the
exam's favorite uniform-convergence trap. The external Klausur bank went fully
local on Jul 2 (link-rot risk closed); Ulm has no solutions — use it as the
timed mock.
